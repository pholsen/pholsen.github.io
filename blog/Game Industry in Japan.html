The Role of Game Industry in Japan's Public Diplomacy

New Media and Public Relations Course Report

Prepared by Kadir Cem Yilmaz

Abstract

In 21st century diplomacy, public diplomacy stands out as an important tool. Since public diplomacy is shaped by the use of media based on modern technologies, the development of communication technologies also affects the forms of this type of diplomacy. Younger generations, as the fastest adopters of these technologies, are at the center of global cultural interactions. Today, the influence of popular culture is particularly evident in Japanese products such as anime, manga and video games. This study examines the role of the Japanese gaming industry in the context of soft power and considers the impact of these products on global diplomacy. Games such as Pokémon, Final Fantasy, and The Legend of Zelda have become an effective tool in international relations by introducing Japanese culture and innovation to millions of people around the world. Moreover, games such as Ghost of Tsushima have contributed to the spread of cultural heritage by combining samurai culture and Japanese history with modern gaming technologies.

Keywords: soft power, Japanese gaming industry, cultural diplomacy, Nintendo, Sony.

1. Introduction

In 21st century global affairs, the concept of power is not just about military or economic pressure. Soft power, as defined by Joseph Nye, "It is the ability to get what you want through attraction" (Nye 2004, 10). "A country can achieve what it wants through admiration of other countries' values, emulation of their example, and envy of their openness," Nye explains (Nye 2004, 5).

Japan's video game industry provides a modern example of this concept. With its cultural heritage and technological innovations, Japan has influenced millions of people around the world and has become a global center of attraction. The Japanese gaming industry is not only an economic success story, but also one of the most influential elements of Japan's soft power. Game franchises such as Pokémon and Mario have become global phenomena, while Final Fantasy, Legend of Zelda, and others have demonstrated Japan's mastery of storytelling.

As Nye emphasizes, soft power is the ability to get others to want the outcomes you want. Japan's gaming industry has effectively applied this approach, enhancing the country's reputation internationally. In addition, Japan's strategic efforts, such as the "Cool Japan" initiative, have positioned the gaming industry as a tool for global diplomacy. Through this initiative, Japan has not only achieved economic gains, but has also succeeded in bringing its cultural heritage to the world stage. However, Japan's success is not unlimited. Historical tensions, especially in East Asia, and Japan's inward-looking social structure are among the factors limiting the potential of this soft power.

This essay will examine the role of the Japanese gaming industry in Soft Power diplomacy, discuss the impact of the "Cool Japan" initiative, and examine how this industry acts as a bridge in global cultural relations. It will also assess the challenges facing Japan and potential strategies to overcome these challenges. Understanding the impact of Japanese gaming in both the economic and diplomatic spheres is critical to understanding the place of soft power in the modern world.

2. Soft Power and Cultural Exports

Soft power refers to the influence of a country that stems not only from its military or economic strength, but also from its cultural appeal. Japan has used its soft power effectively on the global stage, particularly through cultural products such as video games. These products have promoted Japan's cultural heritage to millions of people around the world, creating a positive national image.

Japan's cultural exports are an important source of its soft power. Game series such as Pokémon, Final Fantasy and Super Mario have not only provided entertainment, but also reflected Japan's cultural values and aesthetic sense. These games have built a large fan base around the world, strengthening Japan's soft power. The Pokémon series, for example, has been released in more than 50 countries and has generated billions of dollars in revenue worldwide.

Video games are an important example of how Japan has created an effective source of soft power through trade. The international success of these games has not only generated economic gains, but also increased Japan's cultural appeal. Younger generations in particular have learned about Japan's values, storytelling and aesthetics through Japanese games. This process demonstrates that cultural products are not only consumer goods, but also effective tools in international relations.

The global success of Japanese video games has a significant impact on Japan's image internationally. Cultural products have created a bond between peoples and strengthened Japan's international image. As Nye notes, soft power is about getting others to admire your values (Nye 2004, 5). Japanese games have increased people's interest in Japanese culture around the world, contributing to the perception of Japan as a modern and innovative country.

For example, series such as Final Fantasy, Yakuza and Persona have showcased the creative aspects of Japanese culture with their complex storytelling and impressive visuals. These games have reached a large fan base not only in Japan but also in Western countries. This is a clear indication of how Japan pursues a soft power strategy through its cultural products.

Japan's success through trade is an important factor supporting its soft power. For example, products such as Sony's PlayStation have become not only commercial successes but also symbols of Japanese technology and innovation. These products show how Japan is a leader in the global market. Moreover, these game consoles have served as a vehicle for representing Japan's technological superiority and design sense around the world.

Japanese video games have created a cultural bridge between peoples by creating a bond between players. These games have become more than just products from Japan; they have also helped people to become more familiar with Japanese culture. For example, games such as The Legend of Zelda have allowed players to connect with Japanese culture by presenting themes that incorporate Japanese mythology and aesthetics. Such games have increased public interest in Japanese culture and strengthened Japan's reputation internationally.

3. Cool Japan Policy

Japan's "Cool Japan" policy was launched as an official initiative in the early 2010s. Under this policy, Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) aimed to increase Japan's cultural exports and strengthen the country's soft power through these exports. Video games, anime, fashion, cuisine and other cultural products were at the center of this strategy. According to METI's foresight, culture-related industries constitute a growing segment of Japan's economy and are gaining broad appeal around the world (METI 2025).

The Cool Japan policy has played a decisive role in Japan's soft power strategy. As Nissim notes, Japan's popular culture has served globally not only as a means of entertainment but also as a diplomatic tool (Nissim 2008). The Pokémon series, for example, not only provided entertainment, but also reinforced Japan's innovative and modern image around the world (Hatakeyama and Kubo 2000).

This policy aimed to make Japan's cultural products more visible on the international stage and was largely successful. For example, products such as anime and video games have reached a wide audience around the world. Brands such as Pokémon, Naruto and Final Fantasy have represented the innovative and creative aspects of Japan internationally. In the process, Japan was perceived not only as a product provider but also as a cultural leader.

The Cool Japan policy not only increased Japan's economic influence in the international arena, but also made an important contribution as an instrument of cultural diplomacy. Japan's cultural products have connected people in different countries and contributed to the perception of Japan as a modern and creative country.

4. Global Dominance of the Japanese Gaming Industry

The Japanese gaming industry provides one of the most powerful examples of how soft power can be used as an effective tool. This success story began with Nintendo's entry into the American market in the early 1980s. Launched in 1983, the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) opened the doors to a new era in the video game industry. The NES was seen not only as a technological achievement, but also as a symbol of Japan's ability to produce innovative and quality products.

Following this success, the Game Boy, released in 1989, brought portable gaming to millions of gamers around the world and established Nintendo as a global brand. The fact that cult games such as Tetris could be played on this device contributed to the Game Boy selling 118 million units worldwide.

In 1994, Sony took Japan's leadership in the gaming industry one step further with the launch of the PlayStation console. The PlayStation 2, launched in the 2000s, became the best-selling game console of all time, selling over 155 million units worldwide. This success once again demonstrated the superiority and innovation of Japanese technology on the global stage.

Nintendo's cult franchises such as The Legend of Zelda, Pokémon and Super Mario have left a deep mark not only in the gaming world but also in popular culture. 5. Conclusion

Japan's gaming industry is one of the most powerful examples of how soft power can be an effective tool in the modern world. The innovative approaches of companies like Nintendo and Sony have not only reinforced Japan's economic success, but also deepened the country's cultural influence internationally. Games such as The Legend of Zelda and Pokémon have become cultural icons, enabling millions of gamers to familiarize themselves with Japanese aesthetics, storytelling and values.

Consoles such as the Game Boy and PlayStation, which have sold millions of units worldwide, have shown that Japan is not only a technology giant but also a culture producer. Nintendo and Sony, both listed in Forbes' "100 Most Valuable Companies in the World", are brands that symbolize Japan's leadership in this field. These companies have succeeded in building not only the gaming industry, but also cultural bridges in international relations.

The global impact of the Japanese gaming industry is not limited to economic benefits. Games such as Ghost of Tsushima have contributed to the preservation and dissemination of cultural heritage, promoting Japanese history and aesthetics. In addition, Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice and Breath of the Wild have once again proven Japan's mastery of storytelling and design.

In conclusion, the Japanese gaming industry is a perfect example to understand the power of soft power in the modern world. This industry has proven that cultural products can speak an international language, build ties between peoples and improve diplomatic relations. Through the gaming industry, Japan is not only a producer of culture, but also a cultural leader in the international arena. In the future, it is clear that the Japanese gaming industry will further consolidate its leadership in the field of soft power through innovative and effective solutions.
